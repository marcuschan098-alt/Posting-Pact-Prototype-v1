C · Four-Seat Structure
Pick a scenario and walk through the conversation
Section C demonstrates how the Career Pact is actually held — not as a one-off form, but as a structured conversation. Each scenario below mirrors a real career event that should trigger a Pact, and the number of seats reflects how much authority and accountability the conversation needs to carry. Select an event to see which seats activate, step through what each person says, and view the document produced at the end.
2-seat PactMember & Career Manager. Routine reviews, aspiration resets, career map updates. The Member states intent; the CM records realistic options. No commitments to specific appointments — just direction and process.
3-seat PactAdds Supervisor or SSO. Triggered when a third actor's input or authority is needed — a posting cycle (Supervisor for unit fit), a financial/contractual milestone (SSO for scheme authority), or a sequencing decision that depends on unit endorsement.
4-seat PactMember, Supervisor, CM, SSO together. Used for binding decisions, contested postings, senior milestones, and parallel-destination commitments. Carries the most authority — and the most accountability to re-pact if circumstances change.

A Career Pact is a co-created commitment, not an absolute contract. Entries are recorded in three firmness levels. Aspirations noted capture the member's stated direction and are not promises. Process commitments are actions each seat controls and will do (e.g., schedule a slot, surface a nomination, support a handover). Conditional outcomes — such as the specific posting, course slot, or appointment — remain subject to operational needs, vacancy, board outcomes, and force-development priorities at the time. If reality diverges from this document, the four seats reconvene and re-pact — silence is not an option.
